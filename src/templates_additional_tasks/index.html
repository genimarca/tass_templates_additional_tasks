About

This task is focused on an emotional categorization in the domain of news articles. A corpus is currently being built from RSS feeds of different online newspapers written in different varieties of Spanish (in particular, Argentina, Chile, Colombia, Cuba, Spain, USA, Mexico, Peru and Venezuela). Then the plan is to tag the information provided by each feed (at least, a headline, and in many cases, a brief summary of the article) into an emotional categorization of good, bad, neutral or controversial news, from the point of view of the general public of each corresponding country. The controversial mark is intended to indicate a discrepancy in the different consideration of good or bad news by one part or another of the citizenship, which is frequent in political news. Additionally, a mark will be added to indicate if the article is ironic in the sense that the headline is confusing or ambiguous about the position of the article editor, which can only be determined by reading the full content of the article.

This task could be considered as a kind of stance classification, on the positioning of the editor or the public of an article on a news content. The task supposes a high level challenge because it has to deal with not only a dimension of polarity of feeling (good vs bad) but it has to be decides in combination with a (pseudo) thematic classification to be able to determine the meaning of the news. For example, the reduction of traffic accidents has a negative feeling because of the accidents, but the context of reducing the numbers makes that these are finally good news.

Tasks

We will provide a training corpus labeled in the different varieties of Spanish, and several subtasks will be proposed (still to be defined) using one or several test corpus, for instance: train only with Spanish from Spain and evaluate each variety; train with the variety of each country and specifically evaluate the test of each country, train with everything and evaluate everything, etc.

The systems presented will be evaluated using the measures of Macro-Precision, Macro-Recall, Macro-F1 and Accuracy.
